# ASC 606 — the five-step model

1. **Identify the contract** with a customer (here: the SSA + its Order Schedules).
2. **Identify the performance obligations** — the distinct goods/services promised
   (e.g. a subscription, a one-off setup fee, ongoing support).
3. **Determine the transaction price** — total consideration the entity expects.
4. **Allocate the transaction price** to each obligation, in proportion to its
   **standalone selling price (SSP)**. List price is usually NOT stated on the Order
   Schedule, so SSP must be determined separately.
5. **Recognize revenue** as each obligation is satisfied — over time (e.g.
   subscription recognized ratably each month) or at a point in time (e.g. setup fee).

## Key terms
- **SSP (standalone selling price)** — what the item would sell for on its own; the
  basis for splitting a bundle price.
- **Allocation** — dividing the bundle's transaction price across obligations by SSP.
- **Revenue schedule** — the month-by-month recognized amount per obligation over the term.
- **Deferred revenue** — billed/collected but not yet recognized; released as the
  obligation is satisfied.
- **Journal entry (JE)** — the accounting record (debit/credit) that posts recognized
  revenue and adjusts deferred revenue.

## Common booking rule
Subscription revenue is typically recognized **monthly, straight-line** over the term.
Setup fees may be recognized at a point in time or spread, depending on the obligation.
Order Schedule terms can override the SSA — always reconcile the two.
